Well, where to begin? This is my introduction. My name is Thomas ("Toma") Officer. I'm a product designer in Brooklyn, NY and I'm currently enrolled in the Robin Hood incubator.

A grid breaks space or time into regular units. A grid can be simple or complex, specific or generic, tightly defined or loosly interpreted. Typographic grids are all about control. They establish a system for arranging content within the space of a page, screen, or the build environment. Designed in response to the internal pressures of content (text, image, data) and the outer edge or frame (page, screen, window), an effective grid is not a rigid formula but a flexible and resilient structure.
